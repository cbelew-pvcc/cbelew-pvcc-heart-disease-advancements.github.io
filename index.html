Does living a sedentary life affect heart health?

To Start off on this topic did you know that since 1950 the amount of sedentary jobs has increased by 83%!  That’s a massive increase and on top of that physical jobs now only make up 20% of the totally work force.  According to Johns Hopkins cardiologist Erin Michos, M.D., M.H.S. all of this inactivity is taking a toll on everyone’s health. But to what extent? Well if we look at a collection of a bunch of large studies that was conductive in 2015. We can see  from the results that sitting for long periods of time can be connected to worse health outcomes. The data showed in those studies that it increased heart disease but that’s wasted the only thing. It also showed a greater chance of diabetes, cancer and death from some other kind of health related disease. So In regards living a sedentary life still can and will increase you chances.  Michos also say’s “Even if you’re doing 30 minutes per day of physical activity, it matters what you do the other 23 hours of the day”. So it is crucial that  we try to stay as active as possible to ensure that we can keep or health in check
